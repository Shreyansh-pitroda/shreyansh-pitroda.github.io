Research: Legged Robots with Locomotion Plasticity

Harpy: Thruster-Assisted Bipedal Platform

Development of a 7kg bipedal robot with dual 5.5kg thrust thrusters achieving 1.5 thrust-to-weight ratio. The platform features custom 3D-printed joints with 30% improved power density, carbon fiber construction, and real-time control at 500Hz using EtherCAT communication. This hardware platform serves as a testbed for validating novel multimodal locomotion strategies combining aerial and terrestrial capabilities.

QP-MPC Framework for Extreme Terrain

Real-time trajectory optimization framework enabling locomotion on 30Â° inclines and 0.2m step discontinuities. The approach uses Quadratic Programming with Model Predictive Control, achieving sub-3ms computation times while enforcing friction cone constraints and optimizing ground reaction forces. Successfully demonstrated on parkour-style environments with rapid velocity changes from -1 to 1 m/s.

Active Friction Cone Control for Wall Navigation

Novel control strategy for vertical surface locomotion where friction constraints become active rather than geometric. By coordinating thrust-ground force regulation, the system achieves bidirectional exit velocities (-0.6 to 0.6 m/s) during wall contact while maintaining no-slip conditions. This enables wall-to-wall climbing in confined spaces, validated through high-fidelity Simscape simulations.

Capture Point Control with Thrust Augmentation

First-ever demonstration of thruster-assisted bipedal walking using novel buoyancy-based capture point control. The system integrates Extended Kalman Filter for IMU/OptiTrack sensor fusion at 500Hz, enabling robust push recovery up to 50N disturbances. The controller improves stability margin by 45% during dynamic maneuvers compared to traditional approaches.
